System Overview

Welcome to the Smart Integrated Water & Asset Management (SIWAM) platform. This dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of your water utility operations, helping to monitor IoT data, manage assets, and ensure equitable water distribution.

Navigate using the sidebar to access detailed information on devices, sensor readings, valve operations, Water Treatment Plant (WTP) monitoring, and more.

Important Note on Features:

  • **GIS Integration**: The GIS Map section provides a placeholder for advanced spatial analytics, including water distribution network analysis, pressure zone management, and leak detection visualization. It would support KML/KMZ, Shapefiles, and integration with mapping services for comprehensive network visualization.
  • **AI Hydraulic Modelling**: This platform is designed to integrate AI-based hydraulic modeling for predictive analytics, operational optimization, and decision support. This includes forecasting water demand, optimizing pump schedules, and simulating network behavior under various conditions.
  • Real-time data is simulated for demonstration purposes.
  • Authentication is handled automatically by the environment for demo purposes.
  • All data is stored in Firestore, adapting from the document's MongoDB mention.

GIS Map Visualization & Spatial Analytics

**GIS Map Placeholder**
This section would feature an interactive map displaying the water distribution network.

It would integrate with spatial data (KML/KMZ, Shapefiles) and potentially mapping services (Google Maps/Earth) to visualize:
- **Water Distribution Network Analysis**: Pipes, nodes, and their attributes.
- **Pressure Zone Management**: Color-coded areas indicating pressure levels.
- **Leak Detection Visualization**: Highlighted areas or points indicating potential leaks based on sensor data and hydraulic models.
- **Equitable Distribution Analysis**: Visual representation of water supply equity across different habitation zones.
- **Hydraulic Simulation Results**: Overlay of model outputs like flow direction and velocity.

Clickable elements would provide detailed information and time-series behavior for specific assets or zones.

Advanced variants would include real-time data overlays, inequity analytics highlighting undersupplied/oversupplied pockets, and integration with AI for predictive spatial insights.
